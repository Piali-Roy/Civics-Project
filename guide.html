How to reduce food waste

  1. Buy locally

    Buying locally will help support businesses near you and create more employment opportunities. Local produce also has less of a carbon footprint then foods from overseas as it does not need to be transported through overseas plane, long truck trips or more.

  2. Buy misfit produce

    A lot of produce at grocery stores is not sold because they are ugly, this causes a lot of food waste. One thing you can do to combat this is buy the ugly looking produce, usually, stores will also sell misfit produce for cheaper and there are online food subscriptions that will send you rejected produce.

  3. Understand expiration dates

    Understanding expiration dates will allow you to use up foods before they need to be thrown away, leaving less food wasted , some times the expiration dates are only recommended, and sometimes they are just put to help stores keep inventory of their products.
    Type of food date label Description
    Best if used by/before Displays the date in which the product has the best flavour, not a saftey date
    Sell-by Displays how long a store should display a product for, is meant more for inventory managment, and is not a saftey date
    Use-by The last date by which the product is at top quality, not a saftey date except when used for infants.

  4. Store food correctly

    Storing food correctly can increase the nutritional value of foods, decrease the chances of cross contamination and can prolong the shelf life of foods which will help prevent them from spoiling and unnecessarily being thrown away. For example bananas ripen other foods faster as it produced more ethylene gasses, to prolong food storage keep bananas away from others fruits and vegetables.

  5. Food planning

    Food planning could be a useful way to not only balance your diet and make sure you are getting enough of a variety of foods. It is also a very good way to reduce your food waste, by keeping track of what you eat and do not you can plan meals based on your preferences, that way you will end up with meals you actually eat and less food waste, this will be better for the environment and your wallet.

  6. Cook only what you need

    Cook only what you think you will eat at a time, it will reduce the amount of left overs you have and encourage you to waste fewer foods. Sometimes people overestimate the amount of food they eat so cooking only what is necessary will reduce portion sizes and be better for your wallet.

  7. Dont immediatly throw away food scraps

    Often times there are many purposes that peels, stems, tops or any other food scraps have, they often can be eaten and if not eaten they can be boiled with water and some seasoning to make vegetable stock. You can start your own mini garden as some plant parts can be used as seed.

  8. Use your leftovers

    A lot of people already have leftovers but a lot of people also forget to use them, they remain in the back of the refrigerator until someone finds them rotting away and dumps them. You should put your leftovers into glass containers so it's visible and you are more likely to remember to use them.

  9. Share with others

    Sharing is a very good way to use up foods, when you accidentally make to much of something, share it with someone else and avoid wasting it. There are a lot of sites and apps online based sharing and getting foods from others.
